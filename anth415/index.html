The Fascinating Discovery of Homo Naledi

Discovery

In October 2013, explorers first unearthed specimens of the now-extinct hominid species Homo Naledi from the Rising Star Cave System in South Africa. Since then, this discovery has yielded the largest collection of hominind fossils ever found in Africa representing a single species. [2]

Two field expeditions occurred to explore the site where Homo Naledi was found, one in November 2013 and the other in March 2014. During these trips, the team uncovered a small supply of faunal bones, along with a great number of human remains. Researchers place the total number of hominin specimens uncovered at 1550, representing at least 15 different individuals of the Homo Naledi species. [1]

Location

The Rising Star Cave System is located in Cradle of Humankind, South Africa. The site is split into two chambers: the Dinaledi chamber and the Lesedi chamber. Both are located approximately 30 meters below ground. [1]

The Lesedi chamber is named for the word "light" in Setswana, a local language. [3] Meanwhile, Dinaledi roughly translates to "Chamber of Stars" in the Sotho language. [2] Homo Naledi follows this linguistic pattern. The word "naledi" means "star" in Sotho, which is a reference to the Dinaledi chamber where the first specimens were discovered. [1]

Skull Features

The skull features of Homo Naledi are one of the most fascinating takeaways from this discovery. When skulls of this hominin were uncovered, they were suprisingly well-preserved by the conditions of the cave. Researchers were able to re-fit nearly all of the skull components. [3]

Shown here is an interactive model of a Homo Naledi skull. Researchers note that this skull is has rather primative dentition and unique cranial morphology compared to both other hominins and anatomically modern humans. [1]
